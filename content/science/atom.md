## Atomic Structure

<img src="/images/atom-models.svg" alt="atom models" style="background: antiquewhite; padding: 1rem; max-width: 600px;"/>

* composed of subatomic particles: protons, neutrons, electrons
* nucleus
  * dense center of the atom
  * contains protons and neutrons
  * holds most of the atom's mass
* electron cloud
  * where electrons are likely to be found
  * makes up most of the atom's volume

| Particle | Charge |           Mass          |
|----------|:------:|:-----------------------:|
| Proton   |  $1+$  | $\approx 1u$ $(1.007u)$ |
| Electron |  $1-$  |    $\approx 0.0005u$    |
| Neutron  |   $0$  | $\approx 1u$ $(1.008u)$ |

## Atomic Mass

* historically used Atomic Mass Unit ($amu$)
* modern version is [Unified Atomic Mass Unit ($u$)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dalton_(unit)) [^1]
* $u = 1.660540 \times 10^{-27} kg$
* the periodic table shows elements' average atomic mass, the weighted average based on abundance of its isotopes
  * ex. Iron has isotopes with % abundance: $\ce{Fe-54}$ at $5.9\%$, $\ce{Fe-56}$ at $91.8\%$, and $\ce{Fe-57}$ at $2.3\%$
  $$
  \begin{align*}
  \text{average atomic mass }&= (54 \cdot 0.059) + (56 \cdot .918) + (57 \cdot 0.023) \\
  &= 55.905u
  \end{align*}
  $$

[^1]: Changed from $amu$ to $u$ in 1961 to solve conflict between physicists and chemists. $amu$ was based on oxygen, when scientists believed all oxygen was oxygen-16. Later, isotopes of oxygen were discovered, confusing the definition of $amu$. New standard $u$ was defined using carbon-12, where $u = 1/12 \cdot \text{mass of }\ce{^{12}_{6}C}$.  $\ce{C-12}$ is the only atom with a whole-number mass ($12u$). https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon-12#History


## Elements

* different types of atoms, each with a unique set of physical and chemical properties
* an element is identified by the number of protons in its atoms
* an elements *atomic number* ($Z$) is equal to the number of protons in its atoms
  * helium: $Z = 2$, 2 protons
  * iron: $Z = 26$, 26 protons
* atomic number also indicates how many electrons are in a "neutral" atom of that element
* in the periodic table, elements are organized by their atomic numbers
  * increasing atomic number left->right and top->bottom
  * elements in the same column tend to have similar physical and chemical properties
* the total mass of an element is calculated based on its number of protons and neutrons
  * $\text{Mass number} = \text{\# of protons} + \text{\# of neutrons}$
  * electrons have very little mass



## Isotopes
* atoms of the same element with a different number of neutrons
* has different mass from other isotopes of the same element
* notation: $\ce{^{A}_{Z}X}$
  * A = mass number (# of protons + # of neutrons)
  * Z = atomic number (# of protons)
  * X = chemical symbol
* another common notation: $\ce{X-A}$

* example: carbon, atomic number 6
  * in nature, mainly composed of 2 isotopes
    * 6 neutrons: $\ce{^{12}_{6}C}$ or $\ce{C-12}$
    * 7 neutrons: $\ce{^{13}_{6}C}$ or $\ce{C-13}$


## Ions

* an atom or [group of atoms](#polyatomic-ions) with a net electric charge, due to gaining or losing electrons
* $\text{ion charge} = \green{\text{\# of protons}} - \red{\text{\# of electrons}}$
* notated as element's symbol with superscript that shows the charge
  * when the charge is $1+$ or $1-$, the $1$ is usually omitted
  * ex. a magnesium ion with a $2+$ charge is written as $\ce{Mg^2+}$
  * ex. a sodium ion with a $1+$ charge is written as $\ce{Na+}$, not $\ce{Na^1+}$
* **cation** - a positively charged ion (_cat-ion_)
  * when an atom loses electron(s)
  * name is the same as the neutral element
    * ex. $\ce{H+}$ is a hydrogen ion
* **anion** - a negatively charged ion (_an-ion_)
  * when an atom gains electron(s)
  * name ends in _-ide_
    * ex. $\ce{Cl-}$ is a chloride ion, $\ce{N^3-}$ is a nitride ion

### Polyatomic Ions

* groups of atoms that stay bonded together while carrying a net electric charge
* made of multiple atoms, but act as a single unit in chemical reactions
* some common polyatomic ions:
  | Name      | Chemical formula |
  |-----------|:----------------:|
  | hydroxide |    $\ce{OH-}$    |
  | ammonium  |    $\ce{NH4+}$   |
  | phosphate |   $\ce{PO4^3-}$  |
